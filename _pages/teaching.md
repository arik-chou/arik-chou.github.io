## University of California, Riverside

### Microbiology Laboratory
**MCBL 121L &nbsp;|&nbsp; Department of Microbiology and Plant Pathology**

*Teaching Assistant — Winter 2025*
*Instructor of Record: Prof. Jason Rothman*

A 3-unit laboratory course serving as the hands-on complement to Introductory Microbiology (MCBL 121). The course trains students in fundamental quantitative and diagnostic microbiological procedures through weekly six-hour laboratory sessions. As teaching assistant, responsibilities included leading pre-lab instruction, supervising bench work and sterile technique, evaluating lab reports and practical assessments, and holding office hours.

**Skills and techniques covered**

- Aseptic and sterile technique; safe handling of bacterial cultures and biohazardous materials
- Diagnostic bacteriology: isolation, streaking, and identification of bacterial unknowns
- Quantitative microbiology: serial dilutions, plate counts, and viable cell enumeration
- Basic virology: viral propagation and plaque assays
- Microbial genetic exchange: transformation, conjugation, and transduction
- Bacterial epidemiology: project-based investigation tracing transmission and outbreak dynamics
- Microscopy: preparation and examination of bacterial smears, Gram staining, and differential staining
- Interpretation of biochemical and diagnostic test results
- PCR amplification of target genes from bacterial isolates
- FASTA sequence analysis and taxonomic identification via BLAST and sequence alignment tools

### Field Mycology: Ecology, Evolution, and Diversity of Fungi
**MCBL 128 &nbsp;|&nbsp; Department of Microbiology and Plant Pathology**

*Teaching Assistant — Winter 2022 & Winter 2024*
*Instructor of Record: Prof. Sydney I. Glassman*

A 4-unit course introducing students to fungal diversity, morphology, ecology, and societal impacts through lectures, laboratory sessions, and field trips. As teaching assistant, responsibilities included supporting weekly laboratory sections, supervising hands-on microscopy and mushroom identification exercises, co-leading full-day field collections at Emerson Oaks Reserve and Palomar Mountain, grading lab reports and field journal entries, and holding office hours.

**Skills and techniques covered**

- Fungal systematics and phylogenetics across major clades (Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, Glomeromycotina, Oomycota, Myxomycota)
- Macrofungal morphology and field identification using dichotomous keys
- Spore print preparation, microscopy, and slide making
- Ecological roles of fungi: mycorrhizal symbionts, decomposers, plant and animal pathogens
- Molecular methods for fungal identification including amplicon sequencing and bioinformatics
- Fungal culturing and DNA extraction techniques
- Citizen science and biodiversity documentation via iNaturalist
- Fungal impacts on society: medicine, food, toxins, and art
